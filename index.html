under this tree 09-19-2025
What if the fruits grew and the season came to an end
supple beings overripe and rotting on the ground
each slightly imperfect fruit, delicious in the end
left to be picked and consumed.
I wait for the perfect bite.

The tree presents a new bounty
this season lesser in quantity than the last.
Yet, I wait-- hoping for perfection despite.

These now taste rotten.

The tree glancing down and this wide eye child
Sitting at its trunk asking
“Why are my fruit not enough for you?”
And it starts to creep to mind, was the perfect one already grown?
Was I paying attention when it happened?

Who ate you?

Tall boy 01-17-2026
Dissonance in our timeline, sweet revival
To its natural conclusion
puzzles and expansion

I will ride this sweet wave
and patch each springing hole on this ship
as mighty and fair
as this water may be

What is there to be scared of?
Blurted out confessions?
Plumbers tape tangled on the threads?

This ship-- so imperfect, yes
She was built in terror
With the two hands who didn’t know what the sea would contain
She was built without the passengers in mind
Boards glued to boards with the overflow leaking out
Steering into a great sea with a water-logged compass

God dammit.
Just let the water in.
The sea is begging to join.
Who am I but a captain longing for fair weather?
A craftsman wishing for a cedar throne?
Who am I but a plumber without her tools?

Whisper your secrets into the sea as you float under that full moon.
Let your letters float infinitely on.

for mom 05-20-2026
She always fell in love with the wrong person
and for that I owe her my life
In rotation,
her image became a bit more opaque

I called my sister
she said she couldn’t remember why something about today moved in her
So, in the murkiest of waters I gave her the news
So, to share with someone one admittance of grief
So, experience breaking her heart one more time

Yet, it's with heartbreak that someone could be so big so full of life and have left without a movement behind her name
I sprint day and day fearing insignificance at the end of a brief life
meditating on images of a stranger and ask them to hold me close
searched for her name all over the world and found only a grave written in a language I will never understand

Google had no results

Last moments with her, held close by a phone call of resolution
No, today I will not work, I rest.
Today I’ll grasp onto the collection of photos in her name and make an alter of ramen, coins, and palo santo
A child without her culture, I am gathering scraps of the ceremonies of grief I watched her partake in
The way she cried over her father, I shall cry over my mother
I shall make her mistakes and then some
I ran so far away from those who hurt her only to be so in pain in her name

Conjunct07-14-2026
Put on your sunglasses,
sat in the patch of grass,
the wind will catch and push the hair in your face.
Strands tangled between your teeth.
Claw them out, it will happen again.
It will happen again.
Feel it.
Feel the grass, it beckons for you
As your breath reaches it's largest pull yet,
let them pull your body closer.
When the roots dig into your skin,
deep enough to leave permanent marks,
sit up. Brush them off.
look at the planets surrounding your sign,
do not let the suns light distract you from what is yours.
Ask them what they want from you.

I'm running, sprinting towards
an equal and opposite counterpart.
I'll toss them to the ground
and bite their neck,
I will remind them,
"in this pain is the joy of play."
